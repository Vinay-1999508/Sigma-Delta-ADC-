# Digital Back-End: RTL Architecture & DSP Implementation

This section of the pipeline contains the Verilog RTL designed to process the high-speed 1-bit Pulse-Density Modulated (PDM) stream generated by the analog front-end. 

The digital logic performs three primary tasks: mathematical decimation (filtering and downsampling), bus-width adaptation (1-bit to 16-bit), and asynchronous serial transmission (UART).

---

## Hardware Target Specifications

* **Target FPGA:** Xilinx Artix-7 (xc7a100tcsg324-1)
* **Master System Clock:** $10\text{ MHz}$
* **Input Data Rate:** $10\text{ Mbps}$ (1-bit continuous stream)
* **Output Transmission:** $115,200\text{ Baud}$ (Standard RS-232 Protocol)

---

## RTL Module Deep Dive

### 1. 3rd-Order CIC Decimation Filter (`cic_filter.v`)
The core Digital Signal Processing (DSP) block is a Cascaded Integrator-Comb (CIC) filter. The architecture was specifically upgraded to a 3rd-order design ($K=3$) to provide a $-60\text{ dB/decade}$ stopband attenuation, which is mathematically required to suppress the $+40\text{ dB/decade}$ quantization noise generated by the 2nd-order analog modulator.

**Data Path Operation:**
1. **Integration (Running at 10 MHz):** The 1-bit input stream feeds into three cascaded integrator stages. These registers continuously accumulate the input at the master clock rate. To prevent arithmetic overflow while capturing the full dynamic range, the internal register widths are expanded to 16 bits based on the bit-growth formula:
   $$B_{out} = B_{in} + K \times \log_2(R) = 1 + 3 \times \log_2(32) = 16\text{ Bits}$$
2. **Decimation (OSR = 32):** A hardware counter tracks the incoming clock cycles. Exactly every 32 cycles, a single-cycle strobe pulses, downsampling the data rate from $10\text{ MHz}$ to $312.5\text{ kHz}$.
3. **Comb Differentiation (Running at 312.5 kHz):** The downsampled data passes through three cascaded comb stages. These stages calculate the backward difference between the current samples and delayed historical samples, effectively acting as a digital low-pass filter.
4. **Data Valid Handshake:** A `data_valid` flag is asserted to notify the top-level manager that a fresh, stable 16-bit Pulse-Code Modulated (PCM) word is ready for capture.

### 2. Top-Level Integration & Data Splitter (`top_level.v`)
The top-level module bridges the wide 16-bit parallel output of the CIC filter with the narrow 8-bit pipeline of the UART transmitter.

**State Machine Execution:**
* **Idle/Capture:** The FSM continuously monitors the filter's execution line. Upon detecting the `data_valid` strobe, it latches the 16-bit word into a protected holding register to prevent mid-transmission data corruption.
* **High Byte Transmission:** The FSM isolates the upper 8 bits (`[15:8]`), drives them to the UART module, and asserts the `tx_start` signal. It then waits for the `tx_active` flag to clear.
* **Low Byte Transmission:** Once the line is free, the FSM shifts the lower 8 bits (`[7:0]`) to the transmitter, pulses `tx_start` a second time, and then returns to the Idle state to await the next filtered sample.

### 3. Parameterized UART Transmitter (`uart_tx.v`)
A fully synchronous, hardware-bounded serial engine that frames the 8-bit payloads and shifts them onto a 1-wire asynchronous communication line.

**Clock Division & Framing:**
To step down the $10\text{ MHz}$ master clock to a $115,200\text{ Baud}$ transmission rate, an internal division parameter is utilized:
$$\text{Cycles Per Serial Bit} = \frac{10,000,000\text{ Hz}}{115,200\text{ bps}} \approx 87$$

The FSM utilizes a standard 4-state architecture (IDLE, START, DATA, STOP). It pulls the line low for exactly 87 clock cycles to frame the Start Bit, shifts the payload out sequentially (Least Significant Bit first), and drives the line high for the Stop Bit. A hardware interlock keeps the `tx_active` flag high during the entire 10-bit frame, preventing external triggers from interrupting an active serial transmission.
